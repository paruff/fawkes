## Purpose of the Study

We are conducting user research to understand how people use the Fawkes Internal Platform and identify ways to improve the developer experience. Your participation will help us:

- Understand user workflows, challenges, and needs
- Identify pain points and areas for improvement
- Validate platform features and capabilities
- Make data-informed decisions about platform development

## How Your Information Will Be Used

### Data Usage

Your responses will be used to:
- Identify patterns and themes across multiple interviews
- Inform platform feature development and prioritization
- Create user personas and journey maps
- Share insights with product and engineering teams
- Measure and improve user experience

### Anonymization and Privacy

- **All data will be anonymized** before sharing
- Your name will be replaced with a role descriptor (e.g., "Senior Platform Engineer")
- Team-specific or project-specific details will be generalized
- Quotes may be used but will not be attributed to you by name
- Raw recordings will be stored securely and accessed only by the research team

### Data Sharing

**Anonymized insights will be shared with**:
- Product team for feature prioritization
- Engineering team for platform improvements
- Design team for user experience enhancements
- Leadership for strategic planning

**Raw data (with your identity) will NOT be shared** beyond the immediate research team.

---

## Data Storage and Retention

### Storage

- **Recordings**: Stored in secure, access-controlled cloud storage (not in public repositories)
- **Transcripts**: Anonymized and stored in research repository
- **Insights**: Synthesized findings stored in product documentation

### Access

- **Research team**: Full access to recordings and transcripts
- **Product and engineering teams**: Access to anonymized insights only
- **Leadership**: Access to summary reports and key findings only

### Retention Period

- **Audio/video recordings**: Deleted after 3 months (or after transcription)
- **Anonymized transcripts**: Retained indefinitely for research purposes
- **Research insights**: Retained indefinitely

### Security

- All data encrypted in transit and at rest
- Access logs maintained
- Regular security audits conducted
- Compliance with company data protection policies

## Your Rights as a Participant

### Voluntary Participation

- Your participation is completely voluntary
- You may decline to participate without any consequences
- There is no penalty for choosing not to participate

### Right to Withdraw

- You may stop the interview at any time
- You may decline to answer any question
- You may withdraw your consent after the interview (within 7 days)
- Withdrawal will result in deletion of all your data

### Right to Review

- You may request a copy of your interview transcript
- You may request corrections to any inaccuracies
- You may request deletion of specific statements

### Right to Ask Questions

- You may ask questions before, during, or after the interview
- You may request clarification about how your data will be used
- You may contact the research team at any time

## Additional Notes for Researchers

### Before the Interview
- [ ] Send consent form to participant 24 hours in advance
- [ ] Review consent form at start of interview
- [ ] Answer any participant questions
- [ ] Obtain signature or verbal consent
- [ ] Start recording only after consent is given

### During the Interview
- [ ] Announce when recording starts
- [ ] Remind participant they can stop at any time
- [ ] Respect any topics participant declines to discuss

### After the Interview
- [ ] Store signed consent form securely
- [ ] Note consent status in interview metadata
- [ ] Honor any special requests (e.g., "don't record specific parts")

### Withdrawal Process
If a participant withdraws consent:
1. Stop using their data immediately
2. Delete recordings and transcripts within 7 days
3. Remove any quotes or insights attributed to them
4. Notify participant when deletion is complete
5. Document the withdrawal in research logs
